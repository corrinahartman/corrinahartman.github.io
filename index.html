Albums

Taylor Swift

Debut is Taylor Swift's first full length album and is what started her career. The album is fun, young, and country. Very country. Soulful songs like Teardrops on my guitar show the emotional depth she possesed at a young age, and instant hits like Our Song had everyone in love with Taylor from the beginning.

Fearless (Taylor's Version)

Fearless is Taylor's second studio album, but was her first re-record. No album has upgrade as much as fearless has by her rerecords. The album solidified Taylor as a star with classics like Love Story and You Belong With Me. Still two of her most popular songs. With a pop/country mix, Fearless is phenomenal.

Speak Now (Taylor's Version)

Speak Now is where Taylor shows her song writting talent. The album is solely written by her, and boasts songs that are deeply personal and emotional. Taylor had gone through a lot of public ridicule, and this was her expression of what had occurred. Songs like Dear John, Innocent and Last Kiss are some of her saddest and most gut wrenching songs to this day.

Red (Taylor's Version)

Red (Taylor's Version) is the it girl. No rerecord has had the attention that Red got. Taylor's journey into pop began with Red, which had huge commercial hits, and with the rerecord, All Too Well 10 minute version got it's time to shine. Winning best music video, ATW 10 minute version is wildly regarded as Taylor's best song.

1989 (Taylor's Version)

The pop icon album. You cannot escape her and why would you want to, 1989 is full of HITS. Her biggest songs to date such as Blank Space, Shake it Off and Style can all be found on 1989 (her birth year)

Reputation

Taylor went dark for a full two years, she was not seen in public, no new music, nothing. And then 2016 hit and she dropped Look What You Made Me Do, her decisive and shocking return to the scene. Reputation tells a story about falling in love in private, and sharing how public opinion affects someone.

Lover

The album about being in love. But it is more than that. It's the fun freedom that love can bring, but also the miscommunication and struggles in a relationship. Not all romantic. This album has range, and while it is not the most celebrated of her albums, songs like Cruel Summer are getting their time to shine.

folklore

Covid might have sucked... But it gave us folklore. Taylor takes a pause from telling her own stories and instead shows her song writting skills by writting about fictional situations. This album is lyrically Taylor's best work, and while not as upbeat, it is arguably her best work.

evermore

The sister album to folklore, it was not as popular at the time, but had become a fan favorite. Taylor shows off more of her song writting and talent as a singer in evermore, specifically in the title track, where her vocals are beutifully highlited. The slower songs masterfully show that Taylor has insane range, from pop to indie to country, Taylor does it all.

Midnights

The album that broke every streaming record imaginable. Midnights is about different midnights in Taylor's life, and it spans a variety of styles, but really finds itself in pop anthems like Karma and Anti-Hero. Taylor does pop in the way Shakespere does plays, its art.
